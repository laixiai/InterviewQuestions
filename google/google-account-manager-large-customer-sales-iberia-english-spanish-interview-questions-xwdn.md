## 10 Typical Account Manager, Large Customer Sales, Iberia (English, Spanish) Interview Questions

### Question 1ï¼šDescribe a time you developed a long-term strategic plan for a key client. What was the situation, what steps did you take, and what was the result?
*   **Points of Assessment**: This question assesses your strategic thinking, client management skills, and ability to drive long-term growth. The interviewer wants to see how you analyze a client's business, set objectives, and execute a plan.
*   **Standard Answer**: "In my previous role, I managed a large retail client whose primary goal was to increase their e-commerce market share. I initiated the process with a deep-dive business review, analyzing their sales data, market position, and past campaign performance. From there, I developed a year-long strategy focused on a full-funnel approach, using a combination of YouTube for brand awareness, Search for intent-based targeting, and Shopping ads for conversions. I presented this plan to senior stakeholders, securing their buy-in with clear forecasts and KPIs. We held quarterly business reviews to track progress and make adjustments. By the end of the year, the client saw a 40% increase in online revenue and a 15% growth in market share, solidifying our partnership."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Providing a vague or unstructured answer. Failing to quantify the results of the strategic plan.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What was the biggest challenge you faced when getting stakeholder buy-in?
    *   How did you adapt the plan when you encountered unexpected market changes?
    *   Which data points were most crucial for developing this strategy?

### Question 2ï¼šHow would you approach a situation where a major client is hesitant to adopt a new Google advertising product or strategy you recommend?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Evaluates your influencing, negotiation, and relationship-building skills. The interviewer wants to see how you handle objections and build trust.
*   **Standard Answer**: "My first step would be to listen and understand the root of their hesitation. It could be budget constraints, a lack of understanding, or previous negative experiences. I would then address their specific concerns with data and case studies, showing how similar companies have succeeded with the proposed solution. I'd suggest starting with a small-scale pilot or A/B test to demonstrate its value with minimal risk. By framing it as a collaborative experiment, we can build a business case based on their own data. The goal is to act as a consultant, guiding them to the right decision rather than pushing a product, thereby strengthening the long-term relationship."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Being overly focused on "selling" rather than problem-solving. Not seeking to understand the client's underlying concerns.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What if the client still refuses after the pilot?
    *   How do you balance pushing for innovation with respecting the client's comfort level?
    *   Describe a time you successfully changed a key stakeholder's mind.

### Question 3ï¼šImagine a key client in the travel industry in Spain wants to expand their operations into other European markets. Walk me through the key pillars of the international growth strategy you would build for them using Google's products.
*   **Points of Assessment**: Tests your knowledge of Google's ad suite, your strategic thinking for internationalization, and your understanding of market dynamics.
*   **Standard Answer**: "For a Spanish travel client looking to expand in Europe, my strategy would rest on three pillars. Second, 'Audience Targeting & Reach,' where we'd use a combination of YouTube to build brand awareness in the new markets and targeted Search campaigns in the local languages to capture intent. Third, 'Performance & Optimization,' leveraging Performance Max campaigns to drive bookings across all of Google's channels efficiently. Throughout the process, we would use robust measurement and attribution to understand the customer journey and optimize our investment for the highest ROI in each new market."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Giving a generic answer not tailored to the travel industry. Forgetting to mention measurement and market research tools.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How would you adjust this strategy for a B2B client versus a B2C client?
    *   What cultural nuances would you consider when launching campaigns in Germany vs. the UK?
    *   How would you measure the success of the brand awareness campaigns?

*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
