## Account Strategist, SBS Engage, Google Customer Solutions, (Greek, English) Job Skill Interpretation

### Key Responsibilities Interpretation
As an Account Strategist, your primary function is to act as a growth driver for a dedicated portfolio of small businesses. You will proactively establish and nurture client relationships through phone consultations and email, becoming their trusted advisor on all things Google Ads. A significant part of your role involves conducting sales pitches and consultations to help clients maximize their return on investment. Beyond day-to-day advice, you are responsible for **driving substantial customer growth by deeply understanding their business objectives and helping them achieve their marketing goals**. This involves identifying new opportunities, managing risks, and developing strategic quarterly plans for your portfolio. Ultimately, your success is measured by your ability to **deliver against ambitious quarterly business and product growth goals**, making you a important contributor to Google's revenue and the success of your clients.

*   **Client Relationship Management**: Building and maintaining strong connections with clients via phone and email is fundamental to providing ongoing strategic advice.
*   **Bilingual Communication (Greek & English)**: Fluency in both languages is essential to effectively manage and communicate with the client portfolio in the designated region.
*   **Advertising Acumen**: A solid background with at least two years of experience in advertising, online media, or marketing is required to understand the client's landscape.
*   **Portfolio Management**: You must be able to own a portfolio of businesses, understand their growth drivers, and manage risks to ensure their success.
*   **Business Development**: The role requires an entrepreneurial drive to identify new growth opportunities for clients and expand their business with Google's solutions.
*   **Goal Achievement**: You must be capable of delivering against ambitious quarterly business and product growth targets consistently.
*   **Strategic Planning**: Developing quarterly plans for your portfolio by identifying growth opportunities and mitigating risks is a core responsibility.

## Beyond Sales: Becoming a Trusted Advisor
A key career trajectory for an Account Strategist at Google is the evolution from a sales-focused professional to a true strategic advisor. While achieving quarterly growth goals is a primary responsibility, long-term success and advancement depend on your ability to build deep, trust-based relationships with your clients. This means going beyond recommending standard Google Ads products and instead, holistically understanding a client's business model, market challenges, and long-term ambitions. By providing insights that help shape their overall business strategy, not just their advertising campaigns, you become an indispensable partner. This consultative approach is what differentiates a good strategist from a great one. Developing this skill set can open doors to more senior roles within Google, such as Senior Account Strategist, Agency Account Strategist, or even moving into sales management or product-related teams where a deep understanding of customer needs is paramount.

## Mastering the Google Ads Ecosystem
The world of digital advertising is in a constant state of flux, and the Google Ads platform is no exception. For an Account Strategist, continuous learning is not just a recommendation; it's a core job requirement. To be an effective advisor, you must maintain an expert-level understanding of the entire suite of Google's advertising products, from Search and Display to Shopping and YouTube. This includes staying ahead of new feature launches, updates to bidding algorithms, and shifts in attribution modeling. A successful strategist dedicates time to internal training, studying for certifications, and analyzing industry trends. This technical expertise allows you to diagnose underperforming campaigns, identify untapped opportunities, and craft sophisticated, multi-channel strategies that give your clients a competitive edge. Your ability to translate these complex technical details into clear, actionable advice is what will ultimately drive results and build client confidence.

## The Future of Small Business Advertising
As an Account Strategist for small businesses, you are on the front lines of major industry shifts. The future of SMB advertising is being shaped by trends like the increasing role of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in campaign optimization and the growing demand for hyper-personalized customer experiences. Understanding these trends is crucial for providing forward-looking advice. For example, helping a small business leverage AI-powered tools within Google Ads can automate complex tasks and unlock performance insights they couldn't find on their own. Furthermore, with the decline of third-party cookies, guiding clients on how to effectively use their first-party data for targeting becomes a critical strategic conversation. An Account Strategist who can not only manage current campaigns but also educate clients on preparing for these future trends will be an invaluable asset, helping them build resilient and sustainable growth engines.

## 10 Typical Account Strategist, SBS Engage, Google Customer Solutions, (Greek, English) Interview Questions

### Question 1ï¼šDescribe a time you persuaded a skeptical client to adopt a new advertising strategy or product. What was your approach?
*   **Points of Assessment**: This question assesses your consultative sales skills, your ability to handle objections, and your use of data and storytelling to influence decision-making.
*   **Standard Answer**: "In a previous role, I managed an e-commerce client who was hesitant to invest in Display advertising, fearing it wouldn't drive direct conversions. I started by acknowledging their concerns and analyzing their customer journey data, which showed a significant drop-off at the consideration stage. I framed my proposal not as a direct sales tool, but as a strategic way to re-engage high-intent users who had visited their site. I presented case studies from similar businesses showing a 15% lift in overall conversions when Display was used for retargeting. By starting with a small, controlled budget and setting clear KPIs, I was able to demonstrate its value, and it eventually became a key part of their ongoing strategy."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Giving a generic answer without a specific example. Focusing only on the product features rather than the client's business problem.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What kind of data did you use to support your recommendation?
    *   How did you handle the client's specific objections?
    *   What were the final results of this new strategy?

### Question 2ï¼šHow would you approach a client whose Google Ads campaign is underperforming?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Evaluates your analytical skills, problem-solving process, and client management capabilities under pressure.
*   **Standard Answer**: "My first step would be to conduct a comprehensive audit of the account, starting with the client's primary business goals to ensure our campaign objectives are aligned. I would analyze key metrics like Click-Through Rate, Conversion Rate, and Cost-Per-Acquisition to identify specific pain pointsâ€”is it an issue with targeting, ad copy, landing page experience, or bidding strategy? I would then schedule a consultation with the client to discuss my initial findings, ask clarifying questions about any recent market changes on their end, and collaboratively develop a data-driven action plan. I believe in transparency and partnership, so I would set clear expectations for the timeline and potential impact of the proposed optimizations."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Jumping to solutions without a clear diagnostic framework. Blaming external factors without taking ownership of the strategy.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What are the top three metrics you would look at first and why?
    *   How would you communicate bad news to a frustrated client?
    *   Describe a specific tool or report within Google Ads you would use for this diagnosis.

### Question 3ï¼šHow do you stay updated on the latest digital marketing trends and Google product changes?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Assesses your proactivity, commitment to continuous learning, and passion for the digital advertising industry.
*   **Standard Answer**: "I take a multi-pronged approach to stay current. I am an avid reader of industry publications like Search Engine Land and Think with Google to understand macro trends. I also dedicate time each week to completing internal training modules and skill-building exercises on Google's own platforms to stay sharp on new product features and best practices. Furthermore, I believe in learning from my peers, so I actively participate in team meetings and share insights and challenges. This combination of external research, hands-on product training, and collaborative learning ensures I can provide my clients with the most relevant and effective advice."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Mentioning only one source of information. Being unable to name specific publications or learning resources.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   Tell me about a recent trend that you believe will significantly impact small businesses.
    *   Which recent Google Ads update do you find most interesting?
    *   How do you apply what you learn to your client portfolios?

### Question 4ï¼šImagine you own a portfolio of 50 small businesses. How would you prioritize your time and effort to maximize growth across all accounts?
*   **Points of Assessment**: This question tests your portfolio management skills, strategic thinking, and ability to work efficiently at scale.
*   **Standard Answer**: "I would use a tiered approach based on growth potential and risk. I'd segment my portfolio into three main groups: high-potential accounts with significant growth opportunities, stable accounts that require maintenance and efficiency improvements, and at-risk accounts that are underperforming or may churn. My proactive outreach and deep strategic planning would focus on the high-potential tier. For the stable tier, I would implement scalable solutions and conduct regular, efficient check-ins. For the at-risk tier, my immediate priority would be to diagnose issues and implement a recovery plan. This data-driven segmentation allows me to allocate my time strategically, ensuring I'm not just fighting fires but actively cultivating growth where it matters most."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Suggesting a "one-size-fits-all" approach. Describing a purely reactive, "squeaky wheel gets the grease" strategy.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What criteria would you use to define a "high-potential" account?
    *   How would you use technology or automation to manage the stable tier?
    *   How would you balance proactive growth calls with reactive client issues?

### Question 5ï¼šTell me about a time you had to manage a difficult client relationship. What was the situation and how did you handle it?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Evaluates your communication skills, empathy, and ability to de-escalate conflict and rebuild trust.
*   **Standard Answer**: "I once inherited an account where the client had lost trust due to a previous strategist's poor communication. The client was frustrated and skeptical of any recommendations. My first step was to schedule a call purely to listen to their past experiences and business goals, without pitching any solutions. By acknowledging their frustrations and showing genuine empathy, I began to rebuild the foundation of trust. I then proposed a 30-day plan with very clear, modest goals and committed to weekly progress updates. By consistently delivering on my promises and maintaining transparent communication, I was able to turn the relationship around, and they ultimately became one of my strongest advocates."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Blaming the client or the previous strategist. Focusing on the problem rather than the solution and the outcome.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How do you separate professional feedback from personal criticism?
    *   What did you learn from that experience?
    *   At what point would you escalate a client issue to your manager?

### Question 6ï¼šHow would you explain the value of performance marketing to a small business owner who has only ever used traditional advertising?
*   **Points of Assessment**: This tests your ability to translate complex concepts into simple, compelling language and your understanding of the core value proposition of digital advertising.
*   **Standard Answer**: "I would start by relating it to their current efforts. I might say, 'Imagine if with your newspaper ad, you could know exactly how many people saw it, how many called your store because of it, and you only had to pay for the people who showed interest.' I'd explain that performance marketing gives us that power online. We can precisely measure every dollar spent and directly tie it to business outcomes, like store visits, online sales, or phone calls. This isn't about just getting your name out there; it's about driving tangible, measurable results that help you grow your business in a predictable and cost-effective way."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Using too much technical jargon (e.g., CPC, CPA, CTR) without explaining it. Failing to connect the concepts to the business owner's primary goal: making money.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How would you set a realistic budget for their first campaign?
    *   What is the single most important metric they should focus on initially?
    *   How would you handle their concern about the complexity of digital ads?

### Question 7ï¼šDescribe a time you used data to uncover a significant growth opportunity for a client.
*   **Points of Assessment**: Assesses your analytical skills, business acumen, and your ability to turn insights into actionable strategy.
*   **Standard Answer**: "I was working with a local bakery whose campaigns were performing steadily but had hit a plateau. While reviewing their Search Terms Report, I noticed a growing number of queries for 'gluten-free birthday cakes,' a niche product they offered but didn't actively promote. The data showed high intent but our ads weren't specific enough. I proposed creating a dedicated campaign and landing page specifically for their gluten-free offerings. We launched the new campaign, and within the first quarter, it generated a 30% increase in online orders for that product category, opening up a new revenue stream they hadn't fully capitalized on."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Providing a vague example without specific data points. Describing basic optimization rather than a true strategic opportunity.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What tools did you use to analyze the data?
    *   How did you quantify the opportunity for the client?
    *   What was the most challenging part of implementing this new strategy?

### Question 9ï¼šHow do you handle the pressure of ambitious quarterly sales and growth targets?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Evaluates your resilience, time management skills, and your personal strategies for staying motivated and organized in a high-pressure, results-driven environment.
*   **Standard Answer**: "I thrive in a results-oriented environment and view targets as a framework for success. To manage the pressure, I am very methodical in my approach. *   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   Tell me about a time you were behind on a goal. What did you do?
    *   How do you stay motivated during a challenging week or month?
    *   What tools or methods do you use to stay organized?
