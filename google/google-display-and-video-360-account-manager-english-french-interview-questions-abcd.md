## 10 Typical Display and Video 360 Account Manager (English, French) Interview Questions

### Question 1ï¼šDescribe a time you developed a programmatic media strategy for a client that significantly improved their campaign performance. What was the situation, your approach, and the result?
*   **Points of Assessment**: This question assesses your strategic thinking, hands-on experience with programmatic platforms, and your ability to link actions to measurable business outcomes. The interviewer is looking for a clear understanding of client objectives and problem-solving skills.
*   **Standard Answer**: "In my previous role, a retail client wanted to increase online sales for a new product line. Their initial strategy was a broad-reach display campaign with low conversion rates. I analyzed their first-party data and identified a high-value audience segment that had previously purchased similar products. I proposed a sequential messaging strategy in DV360, starting with awareness-focused video ads on YouTube, followed by remarketing with dynamic creatives showcasing the specific products users had viewed. We also layered on in-market audiences to capture new, high-intent users. This data-driven approach resulted in a 40% increase in return on ad spend (ROAS) and a 25% lift in conversions within the first quarter."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Giving a vague answer without specific metrics. Failing to explain the "why" behind the strategic choices. Focusing only on tactics without linking them to the client's business goals.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How did you use the specific features of DV360 to execute this strategy?
    *   What challenges did you face, and how did you overcome them?
    *   How would you adapt this strategy in a cookieless environment?

### Question 2ï¼šHow would you approach a situation where a key client is hesitant to adopt a new DV360 feature, such as automated bidding?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Evaluates your consultative sales skills, ability to influence stakeholders, and your understanding of client relationship management. The interviewer wants to see how you handle objections and educate clients.
*   **Standard Answer**: "My first step would be to understand the root of their hesitation. I would schedule a meeting to listen to their concerns, which could be related to loss of control, budget management, or a lack of understanding. I would then address these concerns directly by presenting case studies and data from similar clients who have seen success with automated bidding. I'd suggest a limited-scope A/B test on a smaller campaign, allowing us to compare the performance of the new feature against their current manual strategy. This approach provides concrete data, mitigates risk, and empowers the client to make an informed decision based on results rather than assumptions."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Being overly pushy or dismissive of the client's concerns. Lacking specific data or case studies to support your recommendation. Failing to propose a low-risk trial or test.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What metrics would you use to prove the effectiveness of the new feature?
    *   What would you do if the initial test results were not positive?
    *   How do you stay updated on new DV360 features and their benefits?

### Question 3ï¼šThis role requires identifying upsell opportunities. Can you give an example of how you've successfully expanded revenue from an existing account?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Assesses your business development acumen, proactive sales skills, and your ability to think commercially. The interviewer is looking for evidence that you can actively drive growth.
*   **Standard Answer**: "I was managing an account for an airline that primarily used DV360 for display remarketing. During a quarterly business review, I noticed they were running separate, large-scale video campaigns directly through YouTube. I identified an opportunity to consolidate their media buying for better efficiency and holistic measurement. I prepared a proposal highlighting the benefits of running their YouTube TrueView campaigns within DV360, such as centralized frequency capping across display and video, and the ability to build sophisticated sequential audience journeys. By demonstrating the potential for improved user experience and media efficiency, I successfully convinced them to shift their video budget into DV360, increasing their platform spend with us by 30%."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Describing account management without a clear example of proactive growth. Suggesting an upsell that doesn't align with the client's objectives. Failing to quantify the impact of the upsell.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How do you typically identify such opportunities in your client portfolio?
    *   What internal resources or teams did you collaborate with to build this proposal?
    *   How did you handle any objections from the client or their agency?

### Question 4ï¼šHow do you ensure you are a strategic partner to your clients rather than just a vendor who executes campaigns?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Tests your understanding of high-level client service, relationship management, and strategic thinking. The interviewer wants to know if you can operate as a trusted advisor.
*   **Standard Answer**: "Becoming a strategic partner requires moving beyond campaign metrics and deeply understanding the client's business. I achieve this by conducting regular, proactive business reviews focused on their broader marketing goals and industry challenges, not just campaign performance. I share relevant industry trends, competitive insights, and platform best practices that they can apply to their strategy. I also make it a point to connect with senior stakeholders to understand their vision. By consistently bringing new ideas to the table and translating their business needs into actionable, innovative solutions on the Google platform, I build the trust necessary to be seen as an indispensable advisor."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Describing standard account management tasks (e.g., "I send reports on time"). Lacking examples of proactive, strategic initiatives. Failing to mention the importance of understanding the client's business beyond advertising.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How do you prepare for a quarterly business review with a major client?
    *   Tell me about a time your strategic advice changed a client's approach.
    *   How do you measure the health of a client relationship?

### Question 5ï¼šIn this role, you'll work with many internal teams. Describe your experience collaborating cross-functionally to deliver a solution for a client.
*   **Points of Assessment**: Evaluates your teamwork and communication skills. The interviewer wants to see if you can navigate a large organization and leverage internal resources effectively.
*   **Standard Answer**: "For a major CPG client launching a new product, a complex, multi-faceted campaign was required. I acted as the central project manager, coordinating with our YouTube specialists to develop a video strategy, our gCare team for technical implementation and troubleshooting, and our measurement leads to set up a brand lift study. I established a clear communication plan with weekly check-ins to ensure all teams were aligned on goals, timelines, and responsibilities. By facilitating this collaboration, we delivered a seamless and integrated campaign that exceeded the client's KPIs for reach and brand recall. My role was to ensure every specialist team's contribution was aligned with the client's overarching strategy."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Providing a generic answer about being a "team player." Not specifying the different teams or your specific role in the collaboration. Lacking a clear outcome or result from the collaborative effort.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How do you handle disagreements or conflicting priorities between internal teams?
    *   What tools or processes do you use to manage cross-functional projects?
    *   How do you ensure the client receives a unified message from Google?

### Question 6ï¼šGiven your understanding of the programmatic landscape, what do you see as the biggest challenge for advertisers in the next year, and how can DV360 help solve it?
*   **Points of Assessment**: This question tests your industry knowledge, forward-thinking, and ability to connect market trends to the value proposition of the DV360 platform.
*   **Standard Answer**: "The biggest challenge is undoubtedly signal loss and the deprecation of third-party cookies, which fundamentally impacts targeting and measurement. Advertisers are concerned about maintaining performance without traditional identifiers. DV360 is well-positioned to address this through several solutions. First, it facilitates the use of first-party data for powerful and privacy-safe retargeting and audience modeling. Second, it offers advanced contextual targeting and AI-driven solutions like optimized targeting, which can find relevant audiences without relying on user-level tracking. Finally, its integration with Ads Data Hub allows for sophisticated, privacy-compliant measurement and analysis. My role would be to guide clients through this transition, helping them test and adopt these cookieless solutions to future-proof their advertising."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Mentioning a trend without connecting it back to the DV360 platform. Showing a superficial understanding of topics like privacy or AI. Failing to articulate a clear strategy for how you would help clients navigate the challenge.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How would you explain the benefits of Ads Data Hub to a non-technical client?
    *   What steps would you recommend a client take today to prepare for cookie deprecation?
    *   Besides privacy, what other major trends are you following in the ad tech space?

### Question 7ï¼šImagine a top campaign is underperforming against its main KPI. What is your process for diagnosing the problem and communicating with the client?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Assesses your analytical and problem-solving skills, as well as your ability to manage client expectations during a difficult situation.
*   **Standard Answer**: "My immediate priority would be to conduct a thorough, data-driven diagnosis. I would start by analyzing the DV360 campaign setup, checking everything from targeting settings and bidding strategies to creative performance and inventory sources. I'd then look at performance trends over time to identify when the dip occurred and cross-reference it with any changes made. Once I have a hypothesisâ€”for example, audience fatigue or an ineffective creativeâ€”I would proactively communicate with the client. I would present my findings transparently, explain the potential causes, and come prepared with a clear action plan with 2-3 concrete recommendations for optimization. Maintaining open communication and a solutions-oriented approach is key to retaining client trust."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Jumping to conclusions without a structured analysis. Blaming external factors without taking ownership. Communicating the problem without offering a solution.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What specific reports or tools within DV360 would you use for this analysis?
    *   How would you handle a situation where the client is responsible for the underperformance (e.g., providing a poor landing page)?
    *   Walk me through the steps you'd take to optimize a line item that is not spending its budget.

### Question 8ï¼šThis role requires fluency in both English and French. Can you describe your experience managing business relationships in both languages? (The interviewer may switch to French for this part).
*   **Points of Assessment**: Directly evaluates your language proficiency and your experience in a bilingual business environment.
*   **Standard Answer**: "I have extensive experience working in bilingual environments. In my current role, I manage a portfolio of clients based in both France and the UK. I conduct all client meetings, presentations, and email communications in their preferred language, ensuring that complex technical and strategic concepts are conveyed clearly and accurately. For instance, I regularly present quarterly business reviews in French to my Paris-based clients and then conduct similar strategy sessions in English with my London teams the next day. This flexibility allows me to build strong, personal relationships and ensure there are no communication barriers to achieving our shared goals."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Overstating language ability. Being unable to discuss complex business topics fluently in the second language. Lacking specific examples of using both languages in a professional context.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   (In French) What aspects of programmatic advertising do you find most interesting?
    *   (In French) Describe a successful campaign you have managed for a French-speaking client.
    *   How do you ensure consistency in your communication and strategy across different regions?

### Question 9ï¼šWhy are you specifically interested in a DV360 Account Manager role at Google?
*   **Points of Assessment**: This question assesses your motivation, your understanding of the role, and your alignment with Google's culture and products.
*   **Standard Answer**: "I am passionate about the intersection of technology and advertising, and DV360 is at the forefront of the programmatic revolution. I am drawn to this role because it is not just a sales or support function; it is a highly strategic position that involves solving complex business challenges for the world's leading brands. I am excited by the opportunity to work with a market-leading platform and to have a bird's-eye view of the massive transformation occurring in the industry. Furthermore, Google's reputation for innovation and its team-oriented culture of shaping the future of advertising in the AI-era is an environment where I am confident I can make a real impact and continue to grow professionally."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Giving generic answers about wanting to work for a big tech company. Showing a lack of specific knowledge about the DV360 platform. Focusing only on what you can gain, rather than what you can contribute.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What Google product or innovation are you most excited about right now?
    *   How do you align with Google's mission and values?
    *   Where do you see yourself in five years within the digital advertising industry?

### Question 10ï¼šHow do you prioritize tasks when managing multiple clients with simultaneous and often competing demands?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Evaluates your project management, time management, and organizational skills. The interviewer wants to know if you can handle the pressure of a fast-paced, client-facing role.
*   **Standard Answer**: "I rely on a structured approach to prioritization. At the beginning of each week, I map out all my client deliverables and internal commitments. I then categorize them using an impact/effort matrix. High-impact, strategic tasks like preparing for a major quarterly review or developing an upsell proposal get dedicated blocks of time. Urgent but lower-impact tasks, like responding to routine queries, are handled in batches to maintain efficiency. For competing demands, I focus on transparent communication. If two clients have urgent requests, I assess the business impact of each, communicate realistic timelines to both, and, if necessary, escalate to my manager or leverage support teams to ensure all critical needs are met without sacrificing quality."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Saying you just work on "whatever is most urgent." Lacking a clear system or framework for prioritization. Not mentioning the importance of communication with clients about timelines.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What project management or CRM tools are you familiar with?
    *   Describe a time you had to tell a client "no" or reset their expectations.
    *   How do you ensure that smaller accounts still receive adequate attention?
