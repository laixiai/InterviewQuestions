### Question 2ï¼šImagine we are seeing a new type of misuse in a generative AI product. How would you structure a project to investigate and mitigate this threat?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Evaluates your project management skills, strategic thinking, and ability to handle ambiguity in a novel threat environment.

### Question 3ï¼šHow would you use statistical hypothesis testing to determine if a change to a safety filter was effective?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Tests your knowledge of statistical concepts and their practical application in an engineering context.
*   **Standard Answer**: "To test the effectiveness of a new safety filter, I would set up a controlled A/B test. The null hypothesis would be that the new filter has no effect on the rate of abusive content, while the alternative hypothesis would be that it reduces the rate. I would randomly assign a percentage of users or queries to the control group (old filter) and the experimental group (new filter). The primary metric would be the violation rate. After collecting sufficient data to ensure statistical power, I would use a two-proportion z-test to compare the violation rates between the two groups. If the p-value is below our significance level, typically 0.05, we would reject the null hypothesis and conclude the new filter is effective. I would also monitor secondary metrics, like user engagement and latency, to check for unintended negative impacts."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Incorrectly defining the null hypothesis; forgetting to mention the importance of sample size or checking for side effects.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What would you do if the results were not statistically significant?
    *   How would you handle seasonality or other external factors in your analysis?
    *   What is a p-value and how do you interpret it in this context?
