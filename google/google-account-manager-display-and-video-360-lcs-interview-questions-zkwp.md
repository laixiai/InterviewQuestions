## 10 Typical Account Manager, Display and Video 360, LCS Interview Questions

### Question 1ï¼šWalk me through your process for creating a quarterly media plan for a large retail client using Display & Video 360.
*   **Points of Assessment**: The interviewer is assessing your strategic thinking, your practical knowledge of DV360, and your ability to align media plans with client business objectives. They want to see if you can structure a data-driven plan from start to finish.
*   **Standard Answer**: "My process begins with a deep dive into the client's business objectives for the quarter, such as driving online sales by a certain percentage or increasing brand awareness for a new product line. I would analyze historical performance data within DV360 and Google Analytics to identify trends and insights. Next, I would define the target audience, leveraging a mix of first-party data, Google's in-market segments, and custom audiences. From there, I'd develop a full-funnel strategy within DV360, allocating budget across different line items for awareness (e.g., YouTube), consideration (e.g., high-impact display), and conversion (e.g., programmatic retargeting). I would then select appropriate bidding strategies and formats for each stage and conclude with a clear measurement plan outlining the KPIs we'll use to measure success and a timeline for regular performance reviews."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Giving a generic answer that isn't specific to DV360 or retail. Failing to mention data analysis or specific audience strategies.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How would you adjust this plan if the client suddenly cut their budget by 20% mid-quarter?
    *   What specific DV360 features would you use to maximize reach for an awareness campaign?
    *   How would you incorporate YouTube into this DV360 plan?

### Question 2ï¼šDescribe a time you identified a significant upsell opportunity with an existing client. How did you pitch it and what was the result?
*   **Points of Assessment**: This question evaluates your business development acumen, consultative sales skills, and ability to demonstrate value to clients. The interviewer is looking for a concrete example of how you drive revenue growth.
*   **Standard Answer**: "In my previous role, I noticed a key fashion client was running successful prospecting campaigns in DV360 but had no strategy for re-engaging users who abandoned their shopping carts. I analyzed their site data and calculated the potential revenue loss. I then developed a proposal for a dynamic remarketing campaign using their product feed to serve personalized ads to these high-intent users. In my pitch, I presented the data on potential revenue recovery and showcased creative mockups. I proposed a two-week test with a small budget. The test exceeded the ROAS target by 50%, and the client subsequently allocated a significant ongoing budget to this strategy, making it a permanent part of their marketing mix."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Lacking a specific, quantifiable result. Describing an opportunity without detailing the steps taken to secure the investment.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What objections did the client have, and how did you address them?
    *   How did you collaborate with other teams (e.g., creative, analytics) to build this pitch?
    *   How do you typically identify such opportunities in your portfolio of clients?

### Question 3ï¼šImagine a high-priority client is unhappy with their campaign performance and is considering pausing their DV360 spend. What are your immediate next steps?
*   **Points of Assessment**: This situational question tests your problem-solving skills, client management abilities under pressure, and technical troubleshooting knowledge.
*   **Standard Answer**: "My immediate first step would be to schedule a call with the client to fully understand their concerns and which specific metrics are not meeting their expectations. Simultaneously, I would conduct a thorough audit of their DV360 account, analyzing pacing, performance trends, audience targeting, bidding strategies, and creative performance. I would investigate for any recent changes or technical issues that could be the cause. In the meeting, I would listen actively, present my initial findings transparently, and propose a concrete, data-driven action plan. This plan might include short-term optimizations, A/B testing new creatives or audiences, and redefining our KPIs to ensure we are aligned on what success looks like. The goal is to restore their confidence by being proactive, data-driven, and solution-oriented."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Jumping to conclusions without asking clarifying questions. Offering generic solutions without mentioning a data audit. Being defensive rather than collaborative.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What if your audit reveals no obvious errors in the campaign setup?
    *   How would you manage internal communication with your leadership about this at-risk client?
    *   What are three common reasons a DV360 campaign might underperform?

### Question 4ï¼šHow do you build and maintain strong relationships with both the client and their media agency, especially when their priorities might differ?
*   **Points of Assessment**: This question assesses your relationship management, communication, and negotiation skills in a complex, multi-stakeholder environment.
*   **Standard Answer**: "Building strong relationships requires establishing myself as a trusted, neutral partner for both the client and the agency. I achieve this through regular, transparent communication with both parties, often setting up joint meetings to ensure everyone is aligned on goals and strategies. I make it a point to understand the unique objectives and challenges of each stakeholder. For the client, it might be business growth, while for the agency, it could be campaign performance and efficiency. My role is to find the common ground and demonstrate how our DV360 solutions can help both parties achieve their respective goals. By providing data-driven insights that benefit everyone and proactively solving problems, I build trust and position Google as a valuable partner to the entire team."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Taking sides with either the client or the agency. Failing to provide a strategy for alignment. Not emphasizing the importance of shared goals.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   Describe a time you had to mediate a disagreement between a client and their agency.
    *   How do you ensure information is shared effectively between all parties?
    *   What value can you provide to an agency that is already an expert in programmatic buying?

### Question 5ï¼šHow do you stay current with the rapidly changing programmatic ecosystem and Google's product updates?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Evaluates your proactivity, passion for the industry, and commitment to continuous learning.
*   **Standard Answer**: "I take a multi-pronged approach to staying current. Internally, I actively participate in all product training sessions, read weekly updates from product teams, and collaborate with our specialist teams who are at the forefront of their respective fields. Externally, I immerse myself in the industry by subscribing to key publications like AdExchanger and Digiday, and following industry thought leaders on LinkedIn. I also make it a point to regularly use the DV360 platform itself to explore new features as they are released. This combination of structured learning and practical exploration ensures I can provide my clients with the most up-to-date and strategic advice."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Mentioning only one source of information. Not connecting the learning back to how it benefits clients. Sounding passive in your learning approach.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   Tell me about a recent industry trend that you find particularly interesting.
    *   How would you explain a complex technical update, like a change in attribution models, to a non-technical client?
    *   Which Google product update has been the most impactful for your clients recently?

### Question 6ï¼šDescribe a complex project where you had to collaborate with multiple internal teams (e.g., product specialists, analysts, gCare) to deliver a solution for a client.
*   **Points of Assessment**: This behavioral question assesses your project management, teamwork, and cross-functional communication skills.
*   **Standard Answer**: "I once worked with a large retail client who wanted to implement a complex data-driven creative solution in DV360, personalizing ads based on weather data and local inventory. This required coordinating with our creative specialists to build the dynamic templates, the platform specialists to ensure the data feeds were correctly integrated, and our gCare team to troubleshoot implementation issues. I served as the central project manager, establishing a clear timeline, defining each team's responsibilities, and hosting regular check-in meetings to ensure we stayed on track. By facilitating clear communication and ensuring all teams were aligned with the client's goal, we successfully launched a highly effective campaign that significantly lifted sales in targeted regions."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Focusing only on your own contribution. Failing to explain the challenge and the final outcome. Not specifying the different teams involved and their roles.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What was the biggest challenge in getting all the teams to work together?
    *   How did you communicate progress and potential roadblocks to the client?
    *   What did you learn from that experience about cross-functional collaboration?

### Question 7ï¼šIn the context of DV360, how would you advise a client on their measurement strategy, especially in a world with increasing privacy restrictions?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Tests your strategic thinking, technical knowledge of measurement and attribution, and understanding of major industry shifts like privacy.
*   **Standard Answer**: "My advice would focus on building a durable, privacy-centric measurement framework. First, I would encourage the client to prioritize the responsible collection and use of their first-party data. Second, I would guide them on implementing Google's privacy-safe solutions, such as enhanced conversions and consent mode, to respect user choice while still measuring performance accurately. Third, I would advocate for moving beyond last-click attribution to more advanced data-driven attribution models within DV360, which provide a fuller picture of the customer journey. Finally, I would emphasize the importance of lift studies (Brand Lift, Conversion Lift) to measure the true incremental impact of their campaigns in a world where individual-level tracking is less prevalent."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Giving an outdated answer focused only on cookies. Not mentioning specific Google solutions. Failing to explain *why* these changes are important.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How would you explain data-driven attribution to a client who only understands last-click?
    *   What are the main benefits of implementing enhanced conversions?
    *   How do you see AI influencing measurement in the next few years?

### Question 8ï¼šHow would you use data to build a compelling narrative and influence a senior executive at a client organization?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Assesses your analytical skills, strategic communication, and ability to tailor a message to a C-level audience.
*   **Standard Answer**: "To influence a senior executive, the narrative must be concise, strategic, and directly tied to their core business objectives, like revenue growth or market share. I would start by synthesizing complex campaign data into a clear, high-level story. For example, instead of focusing on CTR, I would highlight insights like 'Our YouTube campaign drove a 15% lift in brand searches and influenced X amount in incremental sales among our key growth demographic.' I would use clear data visualizations to illustrate the key takeaways and benchmark our performance against industry trends to provide context. The final presentation would focus on the 'so what' â€“ the strategic implications of the data and a clear recommendation for future investment, framed in the language of business impact, not just media metrics."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Presenting a list of metrics without a story. Using technical jargon with a non-technical audience. Failing to provide a clear recommendation.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What's the most important metric to show a Chief Marketing Officer?
    *   Describe a time your data-driven recommendation was challenged by an executive.
    *   How do you ensure the data you're presenting is accurate and reliable?

### Question 9ï¼šThis role co-owns the Zalando DV360 and YouTube plan. What do you know about Zalando's business, and what initial opportunities might you explore for them?
*   **Points of Assessment**: This question tests your preparation, your commercial awareness, and your ability to think strategically about a specific client.
*   **Standard Answer**: "Zalando is a leading European e-commerce platform for fashion and lifestyle, operating in a highly competitive market. A key challenge is standing out and driving loyalty among a broad customer base. Given this, an initial opportunity I'd explore is leveraging the synergy between YouTube and DV360 for full-funnel marketing. We could use YouTube's broad reach for top-of-funnel brand campaigns to build emotional connection, and then use DV360 to intelligently retarget engaged viewers with dynamic display ads showcasing products they've shown interest in. I would also investigate opportunities in Connected TV (CTV) through DV360 to reach audiences in a premium, lean-back environment. Finally, I would want to understand their current use of first-party data to ensure we are maximizing its potential for personalization and customer lifetime value."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Having no knowledge of the client mentioned in the job description. Suggesting generic ideas not tailored to a fashion e-commerce platform. Failing to connect YouTube and DV360 strategically.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How would you approach the first 90 days in managing this specific account?
    *   What are some key seasonal moments for a client like Zalando that you would plan for?
    *   Who do you see as Zalando's main competitors and how might that affect their digital strategy?

### Question 10ï¼šHow do you balance achieving your sales targets with ensuring the long-term success and satisfaction of your clients?
*   **Points of Assessment**: This question explores your professional integrity, client-centricity, and ability to think strategically beyond short-term goals.
*   **Standard Answer**: "I believe that long-term client success is the only sustainable path to consistently achieving sales targets. My approach is to act as a trusted advisor, not just a seller. I prioritize understanding the client's fundamental business goals and only recommend solutions that genuinely help them achieve those objectives. If a certain product isn't the right fit, I'm transparent about it, as this builds trust. I focus on delivering demonstrable ROI and providing exceptional service, which naturally leads to client satisfaction and retention. This, in turn, opens the door to strategic growth opportunities and upselling that is mutually beneficial, ensuring I meet my targets as a byproduct of helping my clients succeed."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Suggesting that sales targets and client satisfaction are in conflict. Lacking a client-centric philosophy. Failing to mention the importance of trust and transparency.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   Tell me about a time you had to push back on a client request that you knew wouldn't be successful.
    *   How do you define "client success"?
    *   What would you do if your sales target required you to sell a product you didn't think was right for the client?
