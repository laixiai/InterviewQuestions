## 10 Typical Account Strategist, SBS Engage, GCS (English, Lithuanian, Latvian or Estonian) Interview Questions

### Question 1ï¼šDescribe a time you successfully pitched a new advertising strategy to a skeptical client. What was the outcome?
*   **Points of Assessment**: This question evaluates your consultative sales skills, your ability to handle objections, and your storytelling capabilities.
*   **Standard Answer**: "In my previous role, I managed an account for a small retail business that was hesitant to invest in Display advertising, believing only Search ads drove value. I started by acknowledging their concerns and analyzing their existing customer data in Google Analytics. I discovered a high percentage of their customers visited competitor sites before purchasing. I built a narrative around reaching these potential customers proactively, presenting a targeted Display remarketing strategy with clear budget recommendations and conservative ROI projections. By framing it as a low-risk experiment, I gained their buy-in. The campaign ultimately increased their overall conversion rate by 15% within the first quarter, and Display became a consistent part of their budget."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Giving a generic answer without specific data or context; failing to explain *how* you persuaded the client; focusing only on the outcome without detailing the strategic process.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What was the client's biggest objection, and how did you specifically address it?
    *   How did you use data to build your case?
    *   If the campaign had failed, what would your next steps have been?

### Question 2ï¼šImagine one of your key accounts is considering reducing their ad spend due to budget cuts. How would you handle this conversation to retain them?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Assesses client relationship management, strategic thinking, and problem-solving skills in a high-pressure situation.
*   **Standard Answer**: "My first step would be to listen and empathize with their situation to understand the full context of the budget cuts. I would then shift the conversation from 'spending less' to 'maximizing efficiency.' I would proactively analyze their account to identify areas of wasted spend or underperforming campaigns that could be paused. Concurrently, I'd highlight the top-performing campaigns that generate the highest ROI, explaining the potential revenue loss if those were cut. I would propose a revised, leaner strategy focused solely on these high-impact areas, demonstrating how we can still achieve core business objectives with a reduced budget. The goal is to position myself as a partner in their financial challenges, not just a vendor."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Immediately becoming defensive or trying to hard-sell; not offering a concrete, data-backed alternative strategy; failing to show empathy for the client's situation.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What specific metrics would you show the client to prove the value of the remaining campaigns?
    *   What if the client insists on pausing all activity?
    *   How would you maintain the relationship during the spending pause?

### Question 3ï¼šHow do you stay updated on the latest trends in digital marketing and Google Ads features?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Evaluates your proactivity, passion for the industry, and commitment to continuous learning.
*   **Standard Answer**: "I take a multi-pronged approach to stay current. I am an avid reader of industry publications like Search Engine Land and Think with Google for high-level trends. For tactical updates, I follow the official Google Ads blog and am an active participant in online forums and communities where advertisers share real-world insights. I also dedicate time each week to exploring the Google Ads interface for new features and beta tests. For example, I've been closely following the evolution of Performance Max campaigns and experimenting with asset group optimization to understand how its AI-driven approach can benefit different business models."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Mentioning only one source of information; giving a vague answer like "I read blogs"; being unable to name a recent trend or feature update.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   Tell me about a recent Google Ads update and how it impacts SMBs.
    *   How do you test and adopt new features for your clients?
    *   Which industry thought leader do you follow and why?

### Question 4ï¼šHow would you prioritize your time and effort when managing a large portfolio of diverse small businesses?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Assesses your strategic planning, time management, and ability to work efficiently at scale.
*   **Standard Answer**: "I would segment my portfolio based on a combination of factors: current ad spend, growth potential, and strategic importance. I'd use a tiered approach. Tier 1 would be high-potential accounts where strategic consultation could lead to significant growth; they would receive proactive, scheduled monthly calls and detailed quarterly business reviews. Tier 2 would be stable, mid-sized accounts that would receive regular check-ins and performance-triggered optimizations. Tier 3 would consist of smaller or self-sufficient accounts, who I'd support with scalable communications like email updates on new features and office hours for questions. This framework ensures that I'm dedicating my most intensive strategic efforts where they can drive the most impact, while still providing excellent service to my entire book of business."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Suggesting a "first-come, first-served" approach; not having a clear system or logic for prioritization; describing a purely reactive workflow.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How would you identify an account's "growth potential"?
    *   What tools or methods would you use to manage this workflow?
    *   How would you handle an unexpected, urgent issue from a Tier 3 client?

### Question 5ï¼šTell me about a time you exceeded your sales or growth targets. What specific actions did you take to achieve this?
*   **Points of Assessment**: This question assesses your drive, results-orientation, and ability to connect actions to outcomes.
*   **Standard Answer**: "In Q3 of last year, my target was a 15% portfolio growth. I exceeded it by achieving 22%. I time-blocked my calendar to ensure I connected with at least five of these high-potential clients each week. The key was a proactive, data-driven outreach strategy rather than waiting for clients to ask about increasing their investment. This systematic approach allowed me to consistently generate incremental growth throughout the quarter."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Attributing success to luck or external factors; failing to specify the actions taken; not quantifying the achievement with specific numbers.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What was the biggest challenge you faced during that quarter?
    *   How did you balance this proactive outreach with your other responsibilities?
    *   Can you walk me through the data you used to identify those opportunities?

### Question 6ï¼šA client's campaign is underperforming. Walk me through the steps you would take to diagnose the problem and propose a solution.
*   **Points of Assessment**: This assesses your analytical skills, technical knowledge of Google Ads, and structured problem-solving approach.
*   **Standard Answer**: "My diagnostic process starts with understanding the definition of 'underperforming'â€”is it a drop in clicks, conversions, or ROI? Next, I'd analyze the competitive landscape using Auction Insights to see if new competitors are driving up CPCs. *   **Common Pitfalls**: Jumping to conclusions without a structured approach; suggesting solutions before diagnosing the root cause; lacking specific examples of what to check within a Google Ads account.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How would you communicate these technical issues to a non-technical client?
    *   What would you do if you couldn't find a clear cause for the underperformance?
    *   Describe the relationship between Quality Score and ad performance.

### Question 7ï¼šHow would you explain the value of a complex product like Google Ads to a small business owner who has never used online advertising?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Evaluates your communication skills, ability to simplify complex topics, and use of storytelling.
*   **Standard Answer**: "I would avoid technical jargon and start with a simple analogy. I'd say, 'Imagine your ideal customer is actively looking for your exact product or service right now. Google Search is like having a storefront on the busiest street in the world, but you only pay rent when someone who is genuinely interested walks in the door.' I would then make it personal to their business. For a plumber, I'd say, 'When someone's pipe bursts and they search "emergency plumber near me," your ad can be the first thing they see.' I would focus on the core value proposition: control over budget, reaching customers with high intent, and the ability to measure clear results. I'd suggest starting with a small, manageable budget to prove the concept and build their confidence."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Overwhelming the client with technical terms like CPC, CTR, and conversions; failing to connect the product to the client's specific business needs; not providing a clear, simple starting point.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How would you address their fear of 'wasting money'?
    *   What is the single most important metric they should focus on initially?
    *   How would you set expectations for results in the first month?

*   **Common Pitfalls**: Describing a situation where you were simply "busy"; not explaining the system or method used for prioritization; failing to mention the importance of communication and expectation setting.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What tools do you use to stay organized?
    *   How do you decide which priority comes first when two tasks are equally urgent?
    *   What did you learn from that experience?
