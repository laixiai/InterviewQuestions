## 10 Typical Digital Strategy Lead, Accelerated Growth, Google Customer Solutions (Japanese, English) Interview Questions

### Question 1ï¼šDescribe your experience managing a portfolio of business accounts. How do you prioritize opportunities for growth?
*   **Points of Assessment**: This question assesses your strategic thinking, account management experience, and ability to make data-informed decisions. The interviewer wants to see if you have a structured approach to managing a client portfolio.
*   **Standard Answer**: "In my previous role, I managed a portfolio of 30 accounts in the tech sector. My prioritization process began with segmenting clients based on their growth potential and current investment. I used a matrix that considered factors like industry trends, the client's business health, and their alignment with our core solutions. For high-potential accounts, I would conduct deep-dive quarterly business reviews to understand their upcoming goals and proactively identify opportunities. For the rest of the portfolio, I implemented a tech-touch strategy using automated check-ins and performance reports to maintain engagement while I focused my direct efforts on strategic growth initiatives. This allowed me to consistently hit my growth targets by focusing 80% of my time on the top 20% of opportunities."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Giving a generic answer without a clear framework. Failing to mention data or specific criteria used for prioritization. Focusing only on the largest clients without explaining the strategy for the rest of the portfolio.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How would you handle an important client who is at risk of churning?
    *   What tools or data points do you find most valuable for identifying growth opportunities?
    *   Describe a time your prioritization strategy had to change unexpectedly.

### Question 2ï¼šWalk me through a time you developed and pitched a complex digital advertising solution to a prospective customer.
*   **Points of Assessment**: Evaluates your consultative sales skills, your understanding of customer needs, and your ability to translate technical solutions into business value.
*   **Standard Answer**: "I worked with a mid-sized e-commerce client who was struggling with low conversion rates despite high website traffic. After analyzing their Google Analytics and market data, I identified that their campaigns were too broad. I developed a multi-layered solution that combined remarketing lists for search ads (RLSA) with a dynamic display campaign targeting users who had abandoned their carts. During the pitch, I focused on the business outcomeâ€”recovering lost sales and increasing ROI. I used their own data to forecast a potential 15% increase in conversions. By simplifying the technical aspects and focusing on the financial impact, I was able to get their buy-in and we ultimately exceeded the forecast."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Focusing too much on the technical features of the product. Failing to connect the solution to the client's specific business problem. Lacking a clear, structured narrative (problem, solution, result).
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What was the biggest objection the customer had, and how did you handle it?
    *   How did you measure the success of the solution after implementation?
    *   How would you adapt that pitch for a C-level executive versus a marketing manager?

### Question 8ï¼šDescribe a time you used your problem-solving skills to turn around an underperforming advertising campaign.
*   **Points of Assessment**: Assesses your analytical abilities, your knowledge of advertising platforms, and your resilience in the face of challenges.
*   **Standard Answer**: "A key client's lead generation campaign saw its cost-per-acquisition (CPA) double in a month. My first step was to diagnose the root cause. *   **Common Pitfalls**: The problem or solution is overly simplistic. The answer doesn't demonstrate a logical, data-driven diagnostic process. Not quantifying the result of the intervention.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What other potential causes did you investigate?
    *   How did you communicate this problem and your proposed solution to the client?
    *   What steps did you take to prevent this issue from happening again?
