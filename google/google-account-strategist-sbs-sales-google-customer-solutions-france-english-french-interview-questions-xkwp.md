### Question 1ï¼šImagine a client from your portfolio is new to digital advertising and is very skeptical about investing their limited budget in Google Ads. How would you convince them of the value?
*   **Points of Assessment**:
    *   Evaluates your ability to handle objections and empathize with a small business owner's concerns.
    *   Tests your consultative selling and influencing skills.
    *   Assesses your ability to articulate the value proposition of Google Ads in simple, business-oriented terms.
*   **Standard Answer**:
    "I would start by acknowledging their skepticism, as it's a completely valid concern for any business managing a tight budget. I'd shift the conversation away from 'spending money' to 'investing in growth.' My first step would be to ask questions to understand their specific business goalsâ€”are they looking for more phone calls, store visits, or online sales? Then, I would explain the key advantage of Google Search ads: targeting customers with high purchase intent at the exact moment they are looking for a product or service. I would propose starting with a small, highly-targeted pilot campaign with a clear, measurable objective and a modest budget. *   **Common Pitfalls**:
    *   Jumping straight into technical jargon about CPC, CTR, and Quality Score, which can overwhelm and confuse a novice client.

*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What if the client says they've tried Facebook ads and they didn't work?
    *   How would you set a realistic starting budget for this client?

For the lowest tierâ€”clients who are stable but have limited growth potentialâ€”I would use scaled communication methods, like email updates with performance insights and automated recommendations, to ensure they are supported efficiently. *   **Common Pitfalls**:

*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:

    *   What tools or signals would you use to monitor risks within your portfolio?

### Question 3ï¼šDescribe a time you used data to create a compelling recommendation for a client that led to a significant positive outcome.
*   **Points of Assessment**:
    *   Evaluates your analytical skills and ability to translate data into actionable insights.
    *   Tests your ability to build a persuasive, evidence-based argument.

*   **Standard Answer**:

*   **Common Pitfalls**:
    *   Discussing the data but failing to connect it to a clear business outcome (e.g., "we increased the click-through rate").

*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How did you handle the client's initial hesitation?
    *   What other data points did you consider before making your recommendation?
    *   How do you ensure you're looking at the right data to begin with?

### Question 4ï¼šA client's campaign is underperforming, and they are threatening to pull their budget. Walk me through your immediate steps.
*   **Points of Assessment**:
    *   Evaluates your problem-solving abilities under pressure.
    *   Tests your client management and de-escalation skills.
    *   Assesses your technical knowledge of campaign diagnostics.
*   **Standard Answer**:
    "My first step would be to actively listen to the client's frustrations to make them feel heard and to fully understand their definition of 'underperforming.' Next, I would dive into their Google Ads account to analyze recent changes, auction insights, conversion tracking, and search term reports to identify the root cause. Is it increased competition, a broken landing page, or ineffective ad copy? Once I have a hypothesis, I would schedule a follow-up call within 24-48 hours to present my findings and a clear, actionable plan to turn things around. This plan would include specific optimizations we can make immediately and set clear expectations for the next 2-4 weeks. The key is to be proactive, transparent, and data-driven to rebuild their confidence."
*   **Common Pitfalls**:
    *   Immediately becoming defensive or blaming external factors like "the market."
    *   Offering a generic solution like "let's increase the budget" without diagnosing the core problem.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What if your analysis shows the client's expectations were simply unrealistic?
    *   What are the top 3 things you would check first in an underperforming search campaign?
    *   How would you communicate a technical issue, like a problem with conversion tracking, to a non-technical client?
